# GTM Motion Strategy Framework

A structured, four-layer framework for designing and executing Go-To-Market strategies. Use
this skill to help users define the right motion for their business, identify gaps in their
current GTM approach, or build a complete strategy from scratch.

---

## The GTM Motion Framework: Four Layers

The image and copy describe a layered 3D architecture. Always think in these four layers —
from foundation to top — and address each explicitly in your output.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LAYER 4: Customer Success & Expansion (Keep + Grow / NRR)  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 3: Channels & Levers (The "When" / Delivery)         │
│  • Inbound  • Outbound  • Paid Advertising                  │
│  • Organic/SEO  • Events  • Partnerships                    │
│  + RevOps as the "Operations Glue" across all channels      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 2: Motions (The "How" / Philosophy of Sale)          │
│  • Product-Led (PLG)   • Sales-Led (SLG)                    │
│  • Community-Led       • Partner-Led                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 1: Strategy Foundation (The "What" and "Who")        │
│  • ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)                             │
│  • Messaging & Positioning                                  │
│  • Pricing & Packaging                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The entire system runs as a **Continuous Cycle** of Growth, Expansion, and Renewals (NRR),
looping from Customer Success back into the top of the acquisition funnel.

## Layer 1: Strategy Foundation — The "What" and "Who"

This is the **bedrock**. No motion or channel works without this being defined first.

| Component | Key Questions to Ask |
|---|---|
| **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** | What industry, company size, tech stack, geography? What pain do they have? What triggers their buying decision? |
| **Messaging & Positioning** | What problem do you solve? Why are you better? What's the one-liner? What's the narrative for each persona? |
| **Pricing & Packaging** | Freemium, usage-based, flat subscription, seat-based? How does price align with value delivery? |

**Common mistake**: Teams jump to channel tactics (outbound sequences, paid ads) without a
locked ICP or clear positioning. The result is high activity, low conversion.

---

## Layer 2: GTM Motions — The "How"

A motion is the **philosophy of how a deal closes**. It dictates everything downstream.

### 1. Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- **Core idea**: The product is the primary acquisition and expansion engine.
- **How it works**: Users self-serve into value (free trial, freemium). The product experience
  converts them — no salesperson required at the initial stage.
- **Best fit**: B2C or bottom-up B2B, low ACV, high user volume, strong UX, inherent virality.
- **Examples**: Slack, Zoom, Dropbox, Figma.
- **Metrics to watch**: Activation rate, time-to-value, product-qualified leads (PQLs), expansion MRR.

### 2. Sales-Led Growth (SLG)
- **Core idea**: Humans drive the deal from prospecting to close.
- **How it works**: SDRs prospect, AEs run discovery/demo/close cycles. High-touch, consultative.
- **Best fit**: Enterprise or complex products, high ACV ($10k+), multi-stakeholder buying committees.
- **Examples**: Salesforce, Oracle, Workday.
- **Metrics to watch**: Pipeline coverage, win rate, sales cycle length, quota attainment.

### 3. Community-Led Growth
- **Core idea**: Evangelists and peer trust drive the sale.
- **How it works**: Company builds a community (Slack groups, forums, conferences) where users
  help each other. The product becomes the natural tool the community uses.
- **Best fit**: Developer tools, creator platforms, open-source, horizontal products.
- **Examples**: dbt, HashiCorp, Notion's ambassador programs.
- **Metrics to watch**: Community DAU/MAU, community-sourced pipeline, NPS, brand mentions.

### 4. Partner-Led Growth
- **Core idea**: Third parties (resellers, VARs, referral partners, system integrators) drive the sale.
- **How it works**: Partners bring credibility, relationships, and distribution you don't have.
- **Best fit**: Complex implementations, regulated industries, geographic expansion, ecosystem plays.
- **Examples**: Salesforce AppExchange partners, AWS ISV programs.
- **Metrics to watch**: Partner-sourced revenue %, partner attach rate, partner pipeline.

### Hybrid Motions (Most Common at Scale)
Many mature companies run **PLG + SLG in tandem**:
- PLG gets individual users adopted (bottom-up)
- SLG converts usage signals into enterprise contracts (top-down)
- The trigger: a product-qualified account (PQA) with enough seats/usage signals that a human
  should engage.

## Layer 3: Channels & Levers — The "When" and Delivery

These are the **delivery mechanisms** that serve each motion. Match channels to motions:

| Channel | Best Paired Motion | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Inbound / SEO / Content** | PLG, Marketing-Led | Blog, docs, templates, SEO — let buyers find you. |
| **Outbound (Email/LinkedIn/Cold Call)** | SLG | Proactive hunting; SDR sequences, personalized outreach. |
| **Paid Advertising** | PLG, Marketing-Led | Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, retargeting for high-intent buyers. |
| **Events (Virtual/In-Person)** | SLG, Community-Led | Conferences, webinars, field events — high-signal trust building. |
| **Partnerships & Ecosystem** | Partner-Led | Co-sell, co-market, referral agreements. |
| **Product Virality / In-App** | PLG | Referral loops, sharing features, network effects built in. |

### RevOps: The Operations Glue
RevOps sits **across all channels** as the connective tissue:
- CRM hygiene and data infrastructure
- Lead routing, scoring, and attribution
- Sales automation and tooling
- Forecasting and pipeline visibility
- Alignment between Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success

Without RevOps, channels produce noise, not signal.

## Layer 4: Customer Success & Expansion — "Keep" and "Grow"

Modern GTM doesn't end at the closed-won. **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** is the most
important growth metric for a recurring revenue business.

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Onboarding** | Structured path from purchase → activation → first value moment. |
| **Customer Success Management** | Proactive health monitoring, QBRs, escalation handling. |
| **Expansion Revenue** | Upsell (higher tier), cross-sell (adjacent products), seat expansion. |
| **Renewals** | Churn prevention; identify at-risk accounts before renewal date. |
| **Advocacy & Referrals** | Happy customers become a community-led and partner-led flywheel. |

**NRR benchmark**:
- <100%: Customers are churning faster than you're growing them. Fix before scaling acquisition.
- 100–115%: Healthy SaaS business.
- 120%+: World-class. Expansion revenue compounds the entire business.

## Diagnostic: Choosing the Right Motion

When a user asks "what motion should I use?", walk through this decision tree:

```
1. What is your ACV (Annual Contract Value)?
   - < $1,000: PLG almost always required. Sales-led is too expensive.
   - $1,000–$10,000: PLG + inside sales hybrid often works.
   - $10,000–$100,000: SLG with marketing support. PLG for initial adoption.
   - > $100,000: Enterprise SLG. High-touch, multi-stakeholder, long cycle.

2. How complex is the buying decision?
   - Single user decides → PLG
   - IT + Finance + Business stakeholder → SLG

3. Does the product have inherent virality or network effects?
   - Yes → Layer PLG even in SLG-primary company.
   - No → Focus SLG efforts more heavily.

4. Is there an existing ecosystem or community?
   - Yes → Invest in Partner-Led or Community-Led as a multiplier.
   - No → Build one intentionally or focus on PLG/SLG first.
```

## Common GTM Mistakes to Flag

1. **Wrong motion for ACV**: Expensive sales team selling a $5/month app. Or self-serve website
   for a $200k infrastructure product.
2. **Skipping the foundation**: Running outbound before ICP is locked. High activity, low ROI.
3. **Ignoring post-sale**: Acquiring customers but NRR < 100%. The bucket leaks.
4. **One motion dogma**: Refusing to layer PLG onto an SLG model (or vice versa) as the
   company scales.
5. **RevOps as afterthought**: Channels produce data, but no one can trust the CRM. Forecasts
   are fiction.
