# GTM Motion Comparison: PLG vs SLG vs Community-Led vs Partner-Led

Use this reference when helping users choose between motions or design a hybrid strategy.

---

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Dimension | PLG | SLG | Community-Led | Partner-Led |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Who drives the sale** | The product itself | Sales reps | Evangelists & peers | Resellers, referrers, SIs |
| **Primary buyer entry** | Self-serve / sign-up | Outbound / demo request | Community participation | Partner referral |
| **Ideal ACV range** | $0–$2,000/yr | $10,000–$500,000+/yr | Any (multiplier motion) | Any (multiplier motion) |
| **Sales cycle** | Days to weeks | Weeks to months | Variable | Variable |
| **CAC profile** | Low (product does the work) | High (people-intensive) | Low–Medium | Medium (rev share) |
| **Best product type** | Self-explanatory, instant value | Complex, needs consultation | Horizontal, community-native | Ecosystem-embedded |
| **Virality required?** | High | Low | Medium | Low |
| **Headcount to scale** | Engineering/Product | Sales & Marketing | Community & Developer Rel. | Partnerships team |
| **Key metric** | PQL rate, Activation %, Expansion MRR | Pipeline, Win Rate, ACV | Community DAU, Community-sourced MRR | Partner-sourced %, Partner attach |
| **Classic examples** | Slack, Figma, Dropbox | Salesforce, Oracle, SAP | dbt, HashiCorp, Notion | AWS ISVs, Salesforce partners |

## When to Use Each

### Use PLG when:
- Product delivers clear value within the first session (time-to-value < 10 min)
- You target individual contributors or small teams first (bottom-up)
- You have (or can build) virality or network effects
- ACV is too low to justify a salesperson per deal
- You want to scale without scaling headcount linearly

### Use SLG when:
- The buyer is a VP, Director, or C-suite (top-down)
- Multiple stakeholders are involved in the buying decision
- The product requires onboarding, implementation, or customization
- ACV is high enough to justify human-led sales ($10k+ ARR)
- Competitive differentiation requires consultative selling

### Use Community-Led when:
- Your users are technical (developers, designers, operators)
- Peer trust and word-of-mouth are your best growth levers
- You want to create a moat that's hard for competitors to replicate
- You have patience — community takes 12–18 months to compound
- You can invest in community managers, events, and enablement

### Use Partner-Led when:
- Partners already have the trust and relationships you want to access
- Geographic or vertical expansion is a priority
- Implementation complexity creates a natural SI (system integrator) opportunity
- You want to scale distribution without scaling your own headcount
- Your product plugs into an existing ecosystem (e.g., Salesforce, ServiceNow)

## Hybrid Motion Combinations (Most Common at Scale)

### PLG + SLG ("Product-Led Sales")
**How it works**: PLG gets individuals using and loving the product. Sales identifies
product-qualified accounts (PQAs) — companies with enough usage signal — and converts
them to enterprise contracts.
- **Trigger for handoff**: Number of seats, usage frequency, reaching a limit in free tier
- **Who does it**: Slack → Salesforce (individual → enterprise), Figma, Notion, Atlassian

### SLG + Community-Led
**How it works**: SLG closes initial deals. Community keeps them engaged, enables expansion,
and generates word-of-mouth that feeds the top of SLG funnel.
- **Trigger for investment**: When NPS is high but referral rate is low — structured community
  unlocks latent advocacy
- **Who does it**: Salesforce Trailhead, HubSpot Academy, Gainsight Pulse

### PLG + Partner-Led
**How it works**: PLG provides the product-level viral loop. Partners provide distribution,
especially in markets or verticals where direct reach is weak.
- **Trigger for investment**: When you see organic traction in a geography or vertical but
  lack local sales presence
- **Who does it**: Many horizontal SaaS companies entering EMEA or APAC via resellers

## Motion Fit Scoring Tool

Score each factor 1–3 for your business. Highest total = recommended primary motion.

| Factor | PLG Score | SLG Score | Community Score | Partner Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ACV < $2k | 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 |
| Product has instant value (< 10 min) | 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 |
| Buyer is individual / bottom-up | 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 |
| Technical / developer audience | 2 | 1 | 3 | 2 |
| Complex multi-stakeholder sale | 1 | 3 | 1 | 2 |
| ACV > $25k | 1 | 3 | 1 | 2 |
| Strong partner ecosystem exists | 1 | 2 | 1 | 3 |
| High peer-trust category | 2 | 1 | 3 | 2 |

Add up scores per column → highest total is your primary recommended motion.
